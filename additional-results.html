Additional results for «Rethinking training of 3D GANs»

  1. Anonymous authors

Summary

This page provides additional results for the «Rethinking training of 3D GANs» submission:

  1. Geometry comparison with EG3D on FFHQ 512x512
  2. Multi-view consistency comparison with EG3D on FFHQ 512x512
  3. Extreme view angles comparison with EG3D on FFHQ 512x512
  4. Convergence plot for the reveresed patch scale schedule: lerp[1, r/R, 1 - min(t/T, 1)] on Cats 256x256
  5. Convergence plot for the standard discriminator on Cats 256x256
  6. MLP-based generator training on Cats 256x256
  7. An attempt to train EG3D on the standard FFHQ 512x512
Please, use a recent version of Chrome to view the videos (or save them locally and view in a specialized program).

1. Comparing learned geometry on FFHQ 512x512 between EG3D and our generator.

Our generator models the geometry in the full dataset resolution, which allows it to model high-fidelity details. On those videos, one can observe that our generator models high-frequency details better: 1) our generator has more detailed hair structure; 2) eyes and mouth for EG3D are over-smoothed. It is recommended to view this videos in the full-screen mode: they have the resolution of ~2048x1024 not to omit high-frequency details.

To produce those visualizations, we followed the pipeline from the EG3D repo: 1) generated MRC files of the density field at the 512x512x512 volume resolution; 2) visualized in ChimeraX via the turn and record commands. For EG3D, we set the marching cubes level parameter to 10 (as recommended by the repo). We used step=1 (the minimal value) for surface resolution and full lighting for the both methods. We used the provided original EG3D checkpoint, named ffhq512-128.pkl. Truncation was set to 0.7 for both generators.

2. Multi-view consistency comparison between EG3D and our generator on FFHQ 512x512.

Our generator has perfect multi-view consistency by construction --- up to the randomness in the integral approximation in volumetric rendering. While EG3D relies on a 2D upsampler which breaks it, resulting in the flickering artifacts: especially noticeable in the hair, eyebrows and beard. Moreover, sometimes it changes global structure: for example smiling => non-smiling when moving the camera from front to side view. It is recommended to view this videos in the full-screen mode: they have the resolution of ~1024x512.

To produce those visualizations for EG3D, we used the original EG3D checkpoint for FFHQ 512x512 and their provided gen_videos.py script. For both methods, we used truncation=0.7.

3. Generating images on FFHQ 512x512 with extreme view angles (and comparing with EG3D).

Visualizing extreme viewing angles for our generator and EG3D. To produce those visualizations for EG3D, we used the original EG3D checkpoint for FFHQ 512x512 and their provided gen_videos.py script. For both methods, we used truncation=0.7. We used 3σ (i.e. 3 standard deviations) for the rotation/elevation angles as the extreme points of the camera trajectory. Our method does not lose multi-view consistency. Note that EG3D renders from a closer distance (since it was trained on a "closer"-cropped version of FFHQ), which hides potential artifacts in the back of the head.

4. Convergence comparison with the reversed uniform patch scaling on Cats 256x256.

Convergence plot on Cats 256x256 in terms of log-FID@2k for the proposed beta sampling, and uniform sampling with lerp[1,r/R,1−min(t/T,1)] schedule for different values of T. This sampling strategy can be seen as the reversed one: optimization first focuses on the full range of patch scales smin∼Uniform[r/R,1] at t=0, and then decreases the sampling range to smin∼Uniform(1,1] at t=T. This forces the model to lose high-frequency details which negatively affects its FID.

5. Convergence comparison for the standard discriminator on Cats 256x256.

Convergence plot on Cats 256x256 in terms of log-FID@2k for the proposed beta sampling and uniform sampling for the standard (i.e. not hyper-conditioned) discriminator. Our proposed sampling strategy stabilizes the training and helps to converge faster in this regime as well.

6. Trying patch-wise training on top of a π-GAN-like architecture.

We trained an MLP-based NeRF GAN model with 32x32 patch sizes. The overall training took 1 day on 8 v100 GPUs and the attained FID is 21.46 after 10M processed images. The original π-GAN attains FID of 68.26 after a week of training on 8 v100 GPUs.

7. Trying to train EG3D on the standard FFHQ 512x512.

The original EG3D paper uses a re-cropped/re-aligned version of it. We attempted to train it on the standard FFHQ benchmark, but it did not work out for us out-of-the-box. Here are the results after 11M processed images (~40% of training or ~3 days on 8 V100 GPUs). We used the same hyperparameters for training as in the original EG3D paper (gamma, generator pose conditioning regularization). The attained FID is 24.5 --- for comparison, StyleGAN2 achieves FID of 6.08 after 11M images; our generator --- 14.78. In this way, EG3D needs some tuning to achieve the same performance on the standard FFHQ benchmark. For the camera parameters, we converted our rotation/elevation angles to extrinsics matrices and, following EG3D, used static distance from the origin for the camera position. We also used constant intrinsics from the original EG3D paper.
